Colors

INTRO

Colors are of three types namely:

  • Named Colors
  • RGB: Mix of Red, blue and green
  • HSL: Hue Saturation Lightness

Colors can be either Foreground or Background.
Which can be added by (color: ;) and (background-color: ;) respectively.

Colors can also be represented by hexadecimal(#) which then are referred as hexcolors
they can be either represented by 3 or 6 characters.

+Read about Opacity and Alpha

RGB

RGB is represnted as follows in three numeric values(0 to 255):

h1 {
color: rgb(23, 45, 23); }

Here the the first, second and third values are of red, green and blue respectively.

Hex and RGB both use 3 values for their colors out of 256
With this we can make 256*256*256 = 16,777,216 colors!, very close to 17 of millions of shades!

HSL

Follows Hue-Saturation-Lightness scheme
First value can be between 0 to 360, the second and third are percentages.

  • Saturation refers to the intensity or purity of the color.
  • Lightness refers to how light or dark the color is. Halfway, or 50%, is normal lightness.

Opacity And Alpha

Can be used in RGB, HSL and HEX as the fourth value where RGB and HSLA need to be changed to RGBA and HSLA respectively.

.container { color: rgb {v1, v2 , v3 ,v4} }

Here, v4 is the Opacity which is called as Alpha and accepts value from 0 to 1

color: TDF5S7G00;

In hex the last two are the alphas, which range from 00 to FF
